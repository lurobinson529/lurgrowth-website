About LUR Growth

Building nonprofit infrastructure that holds, no matter who is at the helm.

Latoya Robinson

The Nonprofit Thrive Architect

Latoya Robinson

Latoya Robinson founded LUR Growth with a clear conviction: nonprofits should not have to depend on the endurance of a single leader to sustain their mission. After more than a decade in nonprofit leadership, she saw the same pattern repeat across organizations of every size. Strategy lived in people, not systems. When those people left, transitioned, or burned out, the organization lost its footing.

LUR Growth exists to change that pattern. Latoya works directly with executive directors, boards, and senior teams to build the operational architecture that keeps an organization stable through leadership transitions, funding shifts, and sector-wide disruption.

Approach

Every engagement starts with a clear-eyed assessment. No assumptions, no templates. Latoya listens to how the organization actually functions, identifies where strategy depends on individual effort rather than embedded systems, and builds a path forward that the team can sustain on their own.

The work is calm, structured, and board-ready. It is designed to produce clarity, not dependency.

Background

Latoya has served in executive and senior leadership roles across community development, human services, and advocacy organizations. She has guided nonprofits through crisis stabilization, leadership transitions, strategic planning, and systems redesign. Her work spans direct service organizations, intermediaries, and foundation-supported initiatives.

She is based in Winston-Salem, North Carolina, and works with organizations nationally.

How We Work

Guiding Principles

Systems Over Heroics

Organizations thrive when the infrastructure holds, not when one person carries everything. We build systems that distribute knowledge, accountability, and decision-making authority across the team.

Clarity Before Action

Before we build anything, we listen. Every engagement begins with a thorough assessment of how the organization actually operates, where gaps exist, and what the team can realistically sustain.

Sustainability Over Speed

Quick fixes create new problems. We focus on infrastructure that holds over time, through leadership changes, funding cycles, and the ordinary pressures of nonprofit life.

In Practice

A Recent Engagement

We recently designed and deployed a customized capacity system for a foundation-supported nonprofit managing named fund stewardship. Their team now has automated reporting, real-time fund visibility, and significantly reduced manual administrative load. We remain their ongoing infrastructure partner.
